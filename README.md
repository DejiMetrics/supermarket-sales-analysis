**Description**

Data analysis is valuable not only for driving revenue growth but also for supporting decision-making and optimizing strategies. This project used Microsoft Excel to clean and analyse a dataset of sales information from a superstore and to create a dashboard presenting meaningful insights. The superstore sells office supplies, furniture, and technology products to home offices, corporate clients, and general consumers. The provided dataset included order geography, profit, price, and order dates, but did not include sales figures. The purpose of this analysis was to evaluate the store's growth performance, diagnose areas of concern, and identify potential avenues for significant business growth.


**Business Questions**

Sales Performance & Revenue Analysis

•	What is the total revenue generated by each product?

•	What is the shipping duration per sale, from order placement to shipment?

•	What are the maximum and minimum recorded sale values?

Category & Product Performance

•	Which product category exhibits the highest sales volume?
Regional & Customer Insights

•	Which geographical region contributes the most to overall profitability?

•	Which customers exhibit the highest purchasing frequency or value?

•	Which cities or states record the highest number of orders?

Growth & Trend Analysis

•	What is the growth rate between 2014 and 2017?

**Data Cleaning and exploration**

Upon opening the dataset in Microsoft Excel, a thorough exploration was conducted to gain a comprehensive understanding of its structure. To ensure the dataset's suitability for subsequent analysis, rigorous data cleaning was performed, involving the identification and handling of inconsistencies, missing values, duplicate records, and excess whitespace. Data cleaning was conducted in Microsoft Excel, utilizing functionalities such as sorting, filtering, and duplicate removal.

**Data Analysis**

•	What is the total revenue generated by each product?

The revenue generated by each product was computed in Microsoft Excel by multiplying the price by the quantity and then applying a discount rate. The specific formula used was: = (price*quantity)*(1-discount rate).

•	Which product category exhibits the highest sales volume?

The analysis of sales data reveals the following performance across key product categories. The technology category generated the highest sales value, amounting to $3,491,750, Following closely, the Furniture category recorded sales of $3,229,365, Office Supplies category had the lowest sales among the three, with a total of $3,173,069. This information will be valuable for strategic decision-making, such as allocating marketing resources or focusing on inventory management for the highest-selling categories.

•	Which geographical region contributes the most to overall profitability?

When looking at the sales data across all the cities and years, New York City consistently shows the highest sales figures. In 2017, New York City had sales of $415,462. In 2016, its sales were $241,045. In 2015, sales reached $322,564. In 2014, sales were $188,920.
Across all the years, New York City generated the largest sum of sales compared to the other cities. So, it is imperative to target the top 10 cities, particularly New York City, which demonstrates the highest sales performance. Consequently, further investment in these markets is essential for maximizing potential.

. Due to the categories showing negative growth rate in the tables. The supermarket should look closely to understand why. It could be because the market is full, the product quality is low, or competitors have better prices. Then they should decide what to do next.

.The supermarket should focus on making more revenue in areas that are growing fast. This can be done by increasing stock and improving marketing.

. On checking "total sales by ship mode" Most deliveries are standard class (60%). If the supermarket wants to compete with modern retailers, it should check if offering cheaper same-day or first-class delivery would make customers happier.

In the "total sales by customer" A lot of sales come from a small number of customers. If these customers leave, revenue will drop a lot. The supermarket should create a loyalty program to encourage smaller customers to buy more and reduce this risk.

. Sales change a lot during the year while looking at the "sale by month". They are higher at the end of the year and lower at the start. To keep income steady, the supermarket should run promotions during the slower months (first and second quarters).
